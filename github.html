IU/Information Infrastructure 1/2

In Info Infrastructure 1 & 2, our submissions would be via GitHub folders, which means you can see the various questions we were tasked with throught the two semesters. These tasks include several different questions that got increasingly more difficult as the semester went on, with the intent of teaching us applicable ways to use our newly-found knowledge of various Python elements, modules, and libraries. In Info Infr 1, we moved through standard Python elements, mostly using vanilla Python, and occasionally a module here and there as need be. In Info Infr 2, our focus shifted to the study of libraries, namely NumPy, Pandas, and SciPy. We also began working moreso with pre-generated data provided to us to manipulate, analyze, and draw conclusions from.

Tidbyt Indycar App

One of my biggest passions is racing. Since I was young, my two largest hobbies have been experimenting on the computer and watching racing. During the 2023 Indycar season, I bought and set up a Tidbyt, a 64x32 LED display that has a decently sized developer community, with the company producing the devices allowing for apps to be uploaded and shared among the community. While I was developing an Indycar app of my own, another, more automated solution was uploaded and accepted by the lead developers for public use. I reached out to the developer, discussed the app, and offered assistance with further features, mostly the individual track map designs. My work is now public and viewable on the device, as well as the link below.
